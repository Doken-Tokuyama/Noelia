Teoría del Past Simple

¿Qué es el Past Simple?

El Past Simple es un tiempo verbal que usamos para hablar de acciones que ocurrieron y terminaron en el pasado.

Practica Hablando

Usa el Past Simple en conversaciones reales

Ejemplos:

  • I played football yesterday. (Jugué fútbol ayer)
  • She went to the cinema last week. (Ella fue al cine la semana pasada)
  • We studied English in 2020. (Estudiamos inglés en 2020)

Verbos Regulares

Los verbos regulares forman el past simple añadiendo -ed al verbo base.

Regla básica: +ed

work → worked

help → helped

Termina en 'e': +d

live → lived

dance → danced

Consonante + y: -ied

study → studied

try → tried

Doblar consonante: +ed

stop → stopped

plan → planned

Verbos Irregulares Importantes

Los verbos irregulares tienen formas especiales en past simple que debes memorizar.

Más Comunes

be → was/were go → went have → had do → did say → said

Muy Frecuentes

get → got make → made take → took come → came see → saw

Útiles

think → thought know → knew find → found give → gave tell → told

Negativo y Preguntas

Forma Negativa

Sujeto + didn't + verbo base

I didn't go to school. She didn't play tennis. We didn't watch TV.

Preguntas

Did + sujeto + verbo base?

Did you go to school? Did she play tennis? Did we watch TV?

¡Importante! En negativo y preguntas, el verbo principal va en forma base (infinitivo sin 'to').
