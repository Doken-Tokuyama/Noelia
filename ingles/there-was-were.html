¿Qué significa "There was/were"?

Usamos "There was" y "There were" para indicar que algo existía o estaba presente en un lugar en el pasado.

THERE WAS (Singular)

Para una persona, animal o cosa

There was a cat in the garden. There was a book on the table.

THERE WERE (Plural)

Para varias personas, animales o cosas

There were many cats in the garden. There were books on the table.

¡Tip importante! Fíjate en si lo que sigue es singular o plural para elegir "was" o "were".

Formas Negativas

Para hacer negativo, añadimos "not" después de was/were:

There wasn't

There was not = There wasn't

There wasn't a cat in the garden. There wasn't any milk in the fridge.

There weren't

There were not = There weren't

There weren't any cats in the garden. There weren't many people at the party.

Preguntas

Para hacer preguntas, ponemos was/were al principio:

Was there...?

Was there a cat in the garden? Was there any milk in the fridge?

Were there...?

Were there any cats in the garden? Were there many people at the party?

Respuestas típicas:

Positivas
Yes, there was. Yes, there were.
Negativas
No, there wasn't. No, there weren't.
